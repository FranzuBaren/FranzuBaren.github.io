About

I studied theoretical physics followed by a focus on computational neuroscience before developing an international career in data science and AI. My professional journey is grounded in academic rigor, translational science, and reproducible, impactful AI methods.

As a former academic researcher now managing data science initiatives at the intersection of AI, data regulation, and business, I strive to bridge the gap between technological innovation and meaningful application in complex, evolving environments. I value robust methodology, open science, and transparent, interdisciplinary collaboration.

For more, see my Detailed CV.

Interests

  • Reproducible computational research: Ensuring that methods, code, and data in AI/ML can be independently verified, extended, and trusted across the research community and industry.
  • Mathematical foundation of ML: Deep understanding of probability, statistics, optimization, and linear algebra to design rigorous algorithms and interpret their limits.
  • Data-centric auditing and ethical AI: Developing processes for auditing, validating, and governing data and models, with an emphasis on fairness, robustness, and regulatory compliance in sensitive domains like healthcare and life sciences.
  • Interpretability and transparency of models: Creating models whose reasoning, predictions, and limitations can be explained to both technical and non-technical stakeholders, especially where decision-making impact is high.
  • Collaborative science and knowledge sharing: Engaging with multidisciplinary teams across academia and industry, contributing to open-source software, and sharing ideas in global communities.
  • Benchmarking robust predictive models: Developing and evaluating machine learning models on meaningful, real-world tasks while assessing generalization and practical value.
  • Scientific communication, mentoring, and best practices: Presenting research clearly, mentoring the next generation of scientists, and fostering openness in code, data, and idea exchange.
  • Research software & scientific tools: Designing, building, and publishing software (including notebooks, apps, and libraries) that accelerate discovery and enable rigorous science.
  • Academic-industry partnerships: Bridging theoretical work and industrial impact by driving collaborations and solutions between research institutions and business.
  • Exploring new directions: Investigating generative AI, causal inference, privacy-preserving ML, and civic technology for social good.
